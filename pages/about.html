Our Story

Borders Bike King started in a small workshop in Peebles with one goal — to give Borders riders a proper local bike shop run by people who actually ride. Founder Jamie McKay had been wrenching on bikes for a decade when he opened the doors in 2009, stocking a handful of hardtails and doing service work out of the back.

Word spread fast. The Borders riding scene was growing — Glentress was pulling riders from all over Scotland — and there was a real need for a shop that understood trail riding, not just leisure cycling. We expanded into a full retail unit in 2013, added bike hire in 2015, and launched our clothing range in 2019.

Today we're a team of six, all riders ourselves. Whether you're buying your first mountain bike, prepping a full overhaul before a big trip, or just after a set of brake pads on a Saturday morning — we treat every customer the same way we'd want to be treated at the trailhead.

What We Stand For

These aren't just words on a wall. They're how we run the shop, every day.

Honest Wrenching

We tell you exactly what your bike needs — and what it doesn't. No unnecessary upsells, no padding the labour bill.

Riders First

Every person on our team rides regularly. We only stock and recommend kit we'd actually use ourselves.

Local Roots

We live and ride in the Borders. Supporting local trails, local events, and local riders is at the core of what we do.

Ride Responsibly

We promote trail etiquette, support the Scottish Outdoor Access Code, and encourage sustainable riding habits.
